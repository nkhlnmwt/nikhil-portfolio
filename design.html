1. How have we been shaping up the product at Niro
This project outlines the story of my product experience at Niro so far. Not necessarily a design focused project, as at times we had to rely on other channels too.

problem statement: Users with pre-approved offers were dropping off without activating their Loans/CLs. (3.8% of the users out of the ones that were reached)

Below we are explaining the process that went behind increasing the convertions at the top of the funnel by ~10 percent.

We approached this problem with multiple angles:
1. Let’s try to improve it as much as we can with UI/UX, and then test it with real users, for finding things that are making them stuck.
- First step involved making improving the UI in general, 1. communicating what’s happening better on the screen, 2. using a more consistant design system for trust etc. More on how we arrive at a design system which balances "developer effort" with "enough personalisation" can be found here.
- Since we didn’t see many improvements just based on these interactions. We started talking with users. talked with 35 of them. We targetted the users that were past step one, but then were stuck when it came to claim the loan. (a lot of these insights were things like, they didn’t know what a PL was)
By combining our assumptions and the insights we gained from calls, we were able to arrive at these set of experiments.

Below are 3 of these ideas, after execution


Next step was running these variants for 2 weeks.
After that time, we noticed a 5% improvement from the previous conversion rate.


An experiment to understand if the users are interested in loans.
Since the improvement was not as significant, it made us question about. The quality of data in the set we were provided with. So to verify the same, we ran another experiment where we reduced the interest rate to 12% (similar to competitve NBFCs)
The objective of the experiment was to evaluate whether we are able to get a similar level of interest from the CatagoryA and B as we are able to get from CatC and D.  In the image below you can see some more specific varibles being tracked.


2. Marketing Efforts: let’s make efforts from marketing side.
Since we saw the difference in convertion in CatAB.

We also started sending out different communication and offers to different cohort of users.

Our expectation was an increase of 3% from catagorising and sending out the offers, but instead it resulted in much more.
Instead we saw about 6% more conversations. So we double downed on this, and started catagorising users further, by tagging differnet medium to mixpanel. We observed that the medium that’s working the best. (whatsapp, SMS, IVR). This helped up focus most of the energy towards these mediums.

Finally, we were able to combine the changes in the UX, and idea of segragating users based on catagory, and were able to increase the top of the funnel by ~10%.

5. Cendrol, Information architecture and some UX
Cendrol is a construction company. Cendrol wanted to digitize the packages they were distributing to their leads. the Packages contained information about their different offerings for building a house.

They started doing it with sending out pdfs for the different plans, which was hard for customers to scan through, especially while comparing thier plans. It wasn’t just a bad experience, but also didn’t make business sense since it was harder for them show them the advantages in the “Luxury+” plan compared to the “premium+”.


1. first step was to write down all the information, to make sense of what information belong to what.

one of the outcomes of writing it down was the realisation that a lot of the information was repetative, what needed to be written again and again, and what didnt.



2. started laying it out with keeping in mind that it could be beneficail if we only wrote what differered in the different pricing plans instead of writing everything again.

3. The problem was that there was not enough space to put all the data into all the coloumns.Finally we realised that it will make be much easiar to scan if we did it using a website.
